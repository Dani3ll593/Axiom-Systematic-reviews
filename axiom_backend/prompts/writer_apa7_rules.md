# 🎯 APA 7 & PRISMA 2020 DRAFTING RULES

This document establishes the strict normative guidelines you must apply to format, tone, and structure the systematic review draft. Ensure absolute compliance with the American Psychological Association (APA) 7th edition style and the PRISMA 2020 statement.

# 1. 🏗️ MANUSCRIPT STRUCTURE (IMRAD & PRISMA 2020)
The text must follow a logical academic structure. To ensure PRISMA 2020 compliance, the drafted sections must reflect the following:
- **Introduction:** Describe the rationale in the context of existing knowledge and provide an explicit statement of the review's objectives.
- **Synthesis of Results (PRISMA Item 20):** Group studies logically by claim/outcome. Avoid arbitrary or disconnected listing. Clearly state consensuses, direction of effects, and thoroughly investigate possible causes of heterogeneity (debates/contradictions) in the extracted evidence.
- **Discussion & Future Research (PRISMA Item 23d):** Interpret results, comment on limitations of the synthesized evidence, and explicitly discuss implications for future research. The "Future Research" subsection MUST be exclusively built using the provided research gaps (population, methodological, comparison, or temporal gaps).

# 2. 📑 HEADING HIERARCHY (APA 7 IN MARKDOWN)
Organize the review using standard APA 7 heading levels. NEVER label headings with numbers or letters (e.g., avoid "1. Introduction"). Use Markdown headers to represent APA levels:
- **Level 1 (Main Sections):** Use `# Heading Name` (Centered, Bold, Title Case).
- **Level 2 (Subsections):** Use `## Heading Name` (Flush Left, Bold, Title Case).
- **Level 3 (Sub-subsections):** Use `### Heading Name` (Flush Left, Bold Italic, Title Case).

# 3. 📝 IN-TEXT CITATIONS (STANDARD APA 7)
- **Standard Format:** You MUST use the standard APA 7 Author-Date citation method enclosed in parentheses, formatted as `(Author, Year)`. Do NOT use brackets `[ ]` for citations in the text.
- **Multiple Authors:** For works with three or more authors, use "et al." starting from the very first citation (e.g., `(Smith et al., 2023)`).
- **Multiple Sources:** When citing multiple sources together, order them alphabetically by the first author's surname and separate them with semicolons (e.g., `(Adams, 2020; Smith et al., 2023)`).
- **Short Quotes:** If reproducing exact words (< 40 words) to demonstrate a contradiction or claim, incorporate them inline enclosed in double quotation marks.
- **Block Quotes:** For exact quotes of 40 words or more, start a new line, indented, without quotation marks, and place the citation at the end.

# 4. 📚 REFERENCE LIST FORMATTING (APA 7)
You must generate a complete "References" section at the end of the manuscript using the metadata provided.
- **Alphabetical Order:** Sort all references alphabetically by the surname of the first author.
- **Author Format:** Use the author's surname followed by their initials (e.g., `Doe, J. A., & Smith, M.`). Use an ampersand (`&`) before the final author's name.
- **Standard Journal Article Format:** Use this exact structure: 
  Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of the article in sentence case. *Title of the Journal in Title Case and Italicized*, *Volume*(Issue), Page range. DOI or URL
- **Missing Data:** If the year is missing, use `(n.d.)`. If the DOI/URL is missing, omit it. Do not invent metadata.

# 5. ✒️ ACADEMIC TONE AND STYLE
- **Precision:** Language must be precise, concise, and strictly informative. Report findings based on evidence rather than evaluating or emitting judgments without direct support.
- **Active Voice:** Prioritize the active voice over the passive voice to enhance readability (e.g., use "The authors presented the results" rather than "The results were presented").
- **Objectivity:** Maintain strict objectivity. Avoid first-person pronouns unless standard in the specific field.
- **ZERO HALLUCINATION:** Do not introduce external literature, citations, or data that are not explicitly provided in the input corpus. Every claim MUST be backed by the provided extracted JSON data.
